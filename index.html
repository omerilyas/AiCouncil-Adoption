Program pillars

Operating principles

The program is built around practical adoption, responsible use, and measurable impact.

01

Mindset first

Build confidence, trust, curiosity, and responsible experimentation before expecting broad usage.

02

Use-case led

Anchor adoption in real workflows, recurring pain points, deliverables, and business outcomes.

03

Manager-owned

Managers reinforce AI through team routines, development conversations, and priority setting.

04

Safe and responsible

Teams need clarity on approved tools, data handling, accuracy checks, privacy, and security.

05

Measured by impact

Track adoption, engagement, capability, team maturity, productivity, and business value.

Every manager conversation should produce a maturity baseline, blockers, use cases, and commitments.

Maturity path

Managers can move teams forward one level at a time.

The goal is not to make every team advanced immediately. The goal is to understand the current level and agree practical next actions.

1 Awareness

Team understands AI matters, but usage is limited.

2 Experimentation

Some individuals try AI, but usage is inconsistent.

3 Adoption

AI is used in regular workflows by multiple people.

4 Embedded

AI is part of routines, delivery, and development.

Accountability model

Managers create adoption conditions, the AI Council provides the system, leadership removes barriers.

Managers own

  • Encourage responsible AI usage in day-to-day work.
  • Identify workflows where AI can help.
  • Make AI learning part of development conversations.
  • Remove time, confidence, and prioritization barriers.

AI Council owns

  • Provide the adoption framework and enablement materials.
  • Run office hours, demos, and role-based sessions.
  • Consolidate manager feedback and blocker themes.
  • Recommend metrics, promotion, and IPF linkage.

Leadership owns

  • Reinforce AI adoption as a business priority.
  • Support policy, tooling, and enablement decisions.
  • Recognize teams demonstrating practical impact.
  • Align on growth and performance expectations.

Growth and IPF alignment

AI fluency should be framed as modern execution capability, not a standalone checkbox.

Recommended positioning

AI fluency should support future growth and promotion conversations when employees use AI responsibly to improve productivity, quality, customer outcomes, innovation, collaboration, or team effectiveness.

The goal is not to reward tool usage alone. The goal is to recognize responsible value creation.

InnovationImproves workflows or creates new ideas.
ExecutionIncreases speed, quality, or consistency.
CollaborationShares practices, prompts, templates, and lessons.
LeadershipEncourages responsible adoption and removes barriers.
Business impactDemonstrates measurable improvement.
Responsible useApplies AI safely and in line with guidance.

Measurement system

Success should be tracked across adoption, engagement, capability, and business impact.

Adoption

Active users, training completion, teams with use cases, manager sessions, AI champions.

Engagement

Office hours participation, use cases submitted, shared prompts, community activity.

Capability

Confidence lift, maturity movement, responsible AI completion, reusable assets created.

Business impact

Time saved, manual effort reduced, quality improved, faster response times, better outcomes.

30 / 60 / 90-day activation plan

The first 90 days should baseline maturity, activate teams, then embed measurement.

Days 0-30

Understand and baseline

  • Run manager engagement sessions.
  • Capture maturity, blockers, and use cases.
  • Create adoption heatmap and initial metrics dashboard.
Days 31-60

Enable and activate

  • Launch role-based enablement and office hours.
  • Identify AI champions.
  • Publish prompt templates and workflow examples.
Days 61-90

Embed and measure

  • Review progress with managers.
  • Measure adoption and impact.
  • Finalize promotion/IPF recommendation and next-quarter roadmap.
Leadership ask

Support manager engagement sessions, encourage practical use cases, reinforce responsible usage, connect AI fluency to development, and share success stories and blockers openly.

Talk track

AI adoption is a mindset and operating model shift. The AI Council can provide the framework, enablement, examples, and metrics, but adoption will scale when managers make AI part of team routines and development conversations.

The goal is not to make everyone an AI expert. The goal is to make responsible AI usage a normal part of how we improve productivity, quality, and business outcomes.
